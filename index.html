GULF TRUST CYCLE

About

Gulf Trust Cycle (GTC) is a member-driven financial cooperative initiative being built by members working abroad, guided by shared values of trust, discipline, and collective economic empowerment. GTC is structured in the spirit of Kenyan SACCOs, enabling members to save consistently, access affordable credit, and grow together through responsible investment.

The initiative is actively being developed with a long-term vision of financial stability, transparency, and sustainable wealth creation for its members.

How GTC Works

GTC operates on principles similar to registered SACCOs in Kenya. Members make regular deposits which form the core savings pool of the cooperative. These deposits enable members to access affordable credit while also supporting collective investments.

Credit facilities are offered to members at relatively low interest rates, with flexible repayment periods, subject to agreed terms and member eligibility. Interest earned contributes to the cooperative’s growth and eventual member dividends.

Profits generated through interest and approved investments are shared among members as dividends in accordance with their contributions and participation.

Transparency & Governance

For accountability and transparency, Gulf Trust Cycle operates through a joint account held with Equity Bank, managed by multiple signatories acting on behalf of the members. Members are entitled to view transaction records and financial statements.

GTC is guided by designated elective roles established to represent the collective interests of members. These positions exist to uphold fairness, integrity, and member-centered decision-making rather than individual authority.

Physical audits and financial reports will be regularly presented to members, allowing full verification of records and operations.

Legal Framework

GTC operates in alignment with Kenyan cooperative principles and is guided by the Cooperative Societies Act (Cap 490, Laws of Kenya). In matters of credit default or dispute, applicable legal provisions allow the cooperative to safeguard member funds in a lawful and structured manner.

Members agree to abide by the constitution, bylaws, and legal obligations governing the cooperative.
