Our Mission

Gulf Trust Cycle’s mission is to empower members through disciplined savings, affordable credit, and collective economic growth. We strive to build trust, transparency, and long-term financial stability while fostering a cooperative spirit that benefits all members.

Our Vision

To become a leading cooperative that exemplifies financial integrity, sustainable growth, and collective prosperity. Gulf Trust Cycle envisions a future where every member has access to resources that enable personal and community advancement.

About Gulf Trust Cycle (GTC)

Gulf Trust Cycle (GTC) is a member-based financial cooperative currently being built through shared vision, trust, and commitment among members working and living abroad. Inspired by the proven SACCO model in Kenya, GTC is structured to promote disciplined savings, accessible credit, transparency, and long-term economic empowerment.

GTC is not a short-term initiative. It is a structured financial cycle designed to grow steadily, strengthen members economically, and create collective value through trust, accountability, and shared responsibility.

Objectives

  • Promote disciplined savings among members for long-term financial stability.
  • Provide affordable and fair credit facilities to members in need.
  • Ensure transparency and accountability in all cooperative activities.
  • Foster collective growth and economic empowerment of members.
  • Invest in sustainable assets to strengthen the cooperative’s balance sheet.
  • Encourage member participation and democratic governance.

How GTC Works

Gulf Trust Cycle operates under cooperative principles similar to registered SACCOs in Kenya. Members contribute regular savings which form the core capital of the cooperative. These savings enable members to access affordable credit while maintaining collective ownership of the fund.

Credit facilities are issued at fair and low interest rates, with flexible repayment periods. All interest earned contributes to the cooperative’s growth and is eventually returned to members through dividends, reinvestment, or asset acquisition.

GTC is structured to invest in sustainable assets over time, strengthening the cooperative’s balance sheet and ensuring long-term financial stability for members.

Transparency & Accountability

For transparency and accountability, Gulf Trust Cycle operates through a joint bank account held with Equity Bank. The account has multiple signatories, ensuring that no single individual controls the funds. Members are able to view and track transactions to promote openness and trust.

In addition, physical audits and financial reports are regularly presented to members. Members have the right to physically audit records, statements, and financial activities of the cooperative.

Leadership and operational roles within GTC are designated through member-based, elective processes. These roles exist solely to represent the interests of members and are guided by fairness, integrity, and collective decision-making.

Legal Framework

Gulf Trust Cycle is governed in line with Kenyan cooperative principles and laws, including the Cooperative Societies Act (Cap 490) of the Laws of Kenya. This legal framework provides mechanisms for member protection, credit recovery, and enforcement in cases of default, while safeguarding collective member interests.
