Introduction

Gulf Trust Cycle (GTC) is a savings and credit cooperative currently being built by members united by a shared vision of economic empowerment, responsibility, and collective growth. The idea of GTC grows from everyday conversations, shared challenges, and a strong desire to create a reliable financial institution owned by its members.

GTC is designed to support members through disciplined savings, affordable credit, and transparent management. It is not merely a financial entity, but a long-term commitment to building stability, trust, and opportunity for current and future members.

What Gulf Trust Cycle Is About

Gulf Trust Cycle operates on the same cooperative principles that guide successful Savings and Credit Cooperative Organizations (SACCOs) in Kenya. It is a member-owned institution focused on savings mobilization, affordable credit provision, and long-term wealth creation.

Members save regularly, creating a pool of funds that is used to support lending, investments, and other cooperative activities. Through unity and discipline, GTC enables members to achieve financial goals that would be difficult individually.

How Gulf Trust Cycle Works

As a member, you contribute savings to your personal account within the cooperative. These savings form the foundation of your borrowing capacity and ownership in GTC.

Members qualify for loans of up to three times their savings, subject to cooperative policies and guarantees. Any loan amount exceeding a member’s savings requires guarantors, with each guarantor committing no more than 80% of their own savings.

Loans are offered at low interest rates and repaid over agreed periods, ensuring affordability and sustainability. This system protects members’ savings while enabling access to credit when needed.

Dividends, Investments & Growth

Gulf Trust Cycle invests surplus funds into income-generating assets and services. Profits earned are shared annually among members as dividends, in proportion to their savings and participation.

This approach ensures that members benefit not only from access to credit, but also from the long-term growth and value of the cooperative.

Legal Framework

Gulf Trust Cycle operates in accordance with the cooperative laws of Kenya, specifically under the Co-operative Societies Act, Cap 490 of the Laws of Kenya. This legal framework governs the formation, management, lending, recovery, and protection of members’ funds.

Any member who defaults on obligations is subject to lawful recovery procedures as provided under Kenyan cooperative law.

Gulf Trust Cycle prioritizes transparency in all its operations. The cooperative operates a single joint account with Equity Bank, managed by multiple signatories to ensure accountability.

Members are entitled to view all financial transactions, and physical audits are regularly conducted and presented to members. Members may also physically inspect audit records to confirm the integrity of all cooperative activities.
